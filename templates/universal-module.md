### 4. Behavioral Questions 101

- **Required:** true

Introduction to the STAR method and behavioral interview framework.

**Example Content:**

```
# The STAR Method: Your Answer Framework

Behavioral questions ("Tell me about a time when...") are universal. The STAR method helps you structure compelling answers:

- **S**ituation: Set the scene briefly
- **T**ask: What was your responsibility?
- **A**ction: What did YOU do? (Most important)
- **R**esult: What happened? Quantify if possible.

> "The best predictor of future behavior is past behavior." — Every hiring manager

**Key Insight:** Interviewers ask behavioral questions because they want evidence, not promises. "I would handle it by..." is weaker than "Here's how I handled it..."

```

Even strong candidates fail interviews by making preventable mistakes.

Your questions reveal as much about you as your answers. Ask questions that show you've done your homework and are thinking about the role seriously.

- [ ] "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
- [ ] "What's the biggest challenge facing the team right now?"
